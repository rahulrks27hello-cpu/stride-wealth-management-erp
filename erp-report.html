2. Industry Overview

Why Wealth Management needs an ERP

Wealth Management is a service-intensive financial sector that manages client assets, investment advice, cash allocation, execution support, regulatory controls, service communication and fee billing. Unlike a generic finance system, a Wealth Management ERP must handle both front-office and control-office processes together.

The sector deals with sensitive client records, regulated investment activity and frequent coordination between relationship managers, advisors, dealers, compliance teams, operations teams, finance teams and client service teams. This creates a strong need for a unified ERP that connects all records and removes process gaps.

Front-office focus

Client acquisition, onboarding, advisory meetings, model selection and rebalancing decisions.

Control-office focus

KYC, AML, suitability, mandate checks, exception handling and audit tracking.

Back-office focus

Settlement, documentation, invoicing, collections, statements, review packs and service delivery.

3. Key Business Operations

Core operating activities in Wealth Management

  1. Client acquisition and onboarding through referrals, lead handling, KYC and mandate setup.
  2. Portfolio planning based on client goals, risk appetite, investment policy statements and house views.
  3. Trade execution support through order capture, approvals, dealing and settlement follow-up.
  4. Regulatory and internal control monitoring through AML, suitability, policy checks and audit logs.
  5. Operational servicing through document management, workflow routing, service tickets and SLA tracking.
  6. Fee administration through AUM-linked billing, invoicing, receivables and collection monitoring.
  7. Client reporting through statements, tax packs, performance books, review meetings and service communication.

4. ERP Objectives

Design goals of the proposed ERP

Single client record

Create one client master for advisory, compliance, finance and reporting teams.

Clear information flow

Move data from onboarding to portfolio, orders, operations, billing and reporting without duplication.

Control integration

Keep Risk and Compliance embedded across the full operating chain instead of as a separate afterthought.

Operational speed

Reduce turnaround time for onboarding, approvals, settlement support and reporting delivery.

Audit readiness

Maintain traceable client, order, exception and billing records for review and control purposes.

Professional service delivery

Support a high-touch client model through statements, review packs and service tracking.

6. Module Connections and Information Flow

How the modules are connected

The Stride Wealth Management ERP has been designed as a connected operating chain rather than isolated departments. Each module either creates a core record, enriches that record, checks that record, or delivers an output based on that record.

  1. Market Watch creates the market context used by advisors and dealers.
  2. CRM & Onboarding creates the client master, KYC record and mandate profile.
  3. Portfolio & Advisory uses the client master and market view to create portfolio advice.
  4. Order Management turns approved advice into executable trade instructions and captures execution results.
  5. Operations & Workflow confirms settlement, documents and service actions linked to those trades.
  6. Billing & Finance uses mandate terms, AUM and trade activity to generate invoices and track collections.
  7. Reporting & Service combines all client, portfolio, trade and fee records into client-facing outputs.
  8. Risk & Compliance monitors onboarding, advice, trading, settlement and billing exceptions across all stages.

9. Benefits of the Proposed ERP

Why this ERP design is suitable for the industry

  • It reflects the actual workflow of a Wealth Management company from lead intake to client reporting.
  • It connects front-office, control-office and back-office processes on a shared operating model.
  • It reduces manual duplication by keeping one client master and one trade trail across departments.
  • It embeds compliance and risk controls inside day-to-day processing instead of placing them outside the workflow.
  • It improves professionalism in client servicing by linking billing, reporting and service follow-up.
  • It is suitable for academic submission because it clearly shows modules, submodules and information flow between departments.
